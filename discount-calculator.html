Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate discount percentage?

Discount percentage is calculated as: (Discount Amount ÷ Original Price) × 100. For example, if a $100 item is discounted by $20, the discount percentage is (20 ÷ 100) × 100 = 20%.

How do I calculate final price after discount?

Final price = Original Price × (1 - Discount Percentage/100). For a $100 item with 20% discount: $100 × (1 - 0.20) = $80. Add sales tax if applicable.

What's the difference between discount amount and discount percentage?

Discount amount is the actual dollar value subtracted (e.g., $20 off). Discount percentage is the proportion of the original price (e.g., 20% off). Our calculator handles both automatically.

How do I add sales tax to the discounted price?

Enable the "Include Sales Tax" toggle in the calculator. Enter your local tax rate, and we'll automatically calculate the tax on the discounted price and show you the total amount to pay.

Can I use this for bulk or multiple discounts?

Yes! For multiple discounts (like 20% off, then additional 10% off), calculate them sequentially. First apply 20% to get a new price, then apply 10% to that new price.

How It Works

Our tool uses simple mathematical formulas to calculate discounts:

  1. Discount Amount: Original Price × (Discount Percentage ÷ 100)
  2. Sale Price: Original Price - Discount Amount
  3. Tax Amount: Sale Price × (Tax Percentage ÷ 100)
  4. Final Price: Sale Price + Tax Amount
