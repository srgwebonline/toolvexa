Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate what X% of Y is?

To calculate what X% of Y is, use the formula: Result = (X ÷ 100) × Y. For example, to find 20% of 150: (20 ÷ 100) × 150 = 0.2 × 150 = 30.

How do I calculate percentage increase or decrease?

To calculate percentage change: % Change = [(New Value - Original Value) ÷ Original Value] × 100. A positive result indicates an increase, while a negative result indicates a decrease.

What's the difference between percentage change and percentage difference?

Percentage change compares a new value to an original value (direction matters). Percentage difference compares two values without considering direction: % Difference = (|Value1 - Value2| ÷ Average) × 100.

How do I calculate reverse percentages?

To find the original value when you know the percentage and result: Original = Result ÷ (Percentage ÷ 100). For example, if 75 is 25% of the original: 75 ÷ (25 ÷ 100) = 75 ÷ 0.25 = 300.

Can I use this calculator for discount calculations?

Yes! Use the "What is X% of Y?" calculator for discounts. Enter the discount percentage and original price to find the discount amount, then subtract from the original price to get the sale price.

Is my data safe with this calculator?

Absolutely! All calculations happen locally in your browser. We don't collect, store, or transmit any of your data. Your calculations remain completely private on your device.
