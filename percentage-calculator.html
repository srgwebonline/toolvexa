Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage of a number?

To calculate percentage of a number, use the formula: Result = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Number. For example, to find 20% of 150: (20 ÷ 100) × 150 = 30.

How do I calculate what percentage one number is of another?

Use the formula: Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100. For example, if 25 is what percent of 200: (25 ÷ 200) × 100 = 12.5%.

What's the difference between percentage increase and decrease?

Percentage increase measures growth (new value is higher), while percentage decrease measures reduction (new value is lower). Both use similar formulas but with different order of values.

How do I calculate percentage increase?

Use: Percentage Increase = [(New Value - Original Value) ÷ Original Value] × 100. Example: From 50 to 75: [(75-50)÷50]×100 = 50% increase.

What is percentage difference?

Percentage difference measures the relative difference between two values, regardless of which is larger. Formula: (|Value1 - Value2| ÷ [(Value1 + Value2) ÷ 2]) × 100.
