About This Image Converter Tool

Our Image Converter is designed to be fast, secure, and privacy-focused. Unlike many online converters, all image processing happens locally in your browser - no images are uploaded to any server. This ensures complete privacy and faster conversion times.

This tool uses modern browser APIs (Canvas and File APIs) to convert images between different formats. It supports all major image formats including JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP.

Key features of our Image Converter:

  • 100% browser-based - no data leaves your computer
  • Batch conversion - convert multiple images at once
  • Adjustable quality settings for optimal file size
  • Support for JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP formats
  • Fast processing with real-time progress updates
  • Download individual files or all as a ZIP archive

Use this tool to convert images for web use, social media, documents, or any other purpose without compromising your privacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No, absolutely not. All image processing happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your images never leave your computer, ensuring complete privacy and security.

What image formats are supported?

Our converter supports all major image formats: JPG/JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP. You can convert between any of these formats in any combination.

Is there a file size limit?

We recommend images under 10MB for optimal performance. Larger files may work but could slow down the conversion process. The actual limit depends on your device's memory and browser capabilities.

How many images can I convert at once?

You can convert up to 20 images at once. This limit helps ensure smooth performance and prevents browser crashes. For batch processing of more images, you can split them into multiple conversions.

What does the quality setting do?

The quality setting controls the balance between image quality and file size. Higher values (85-100) produce better quality but larger files. Lower values (50-70) create smaller files but may reduce image quality. The effect varies by format.

Can I convert transparent PNG images to JPG?

Yes, but note that JPG doesn't support transparency. When converting transparent PNGs to JPG, the transparent areas will be filled with white. To preserve transparency, convert to PNG or WebP format instead.
