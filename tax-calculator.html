Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Tax Calculator accurate for FY 2026-27?

Yes, our tax calculator is updated with the latest tax slabs, deductions, and rules as per the Union Budget 2026. We regularly update the tool to reflect any changes in tax laws, making it accurate for the financial year 2026-27.

Which tax regime should I choose?

The choice depends on your income level and available deductions. Generally, if you have significant deductions (over ₹2.5 lakhs), the old regime might be better. For others, especially with incomes below ₹15 lakhs and minimal deductions, the new regime usually offers lower tax liability. Our calculator compares both regimes to help you decide.

What deductions are available in the new tax regime?

The new tax regime offers limited deductions: standard deduction of ₹75,000 for salaried individuals, deductions under section 80CCD(2) for employer's NPS contribution, and certain allowances for differently-abled individuals. Most other deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.) are not available in the new regime.

How is tax calculated for senior citizens?

Senior citizens (60-80 years) get higher basic exemption limits: ₹3 lakhs in both regimes. Super senior citizens (above 80 years) get ₹5 lakhs exemption. Additionally, they get higher deduction limits for medical insurance (80D) and interest income from deposits under the old regime.

Is my financial data safe?

Absolutely! All calculations are performed locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your income and deduction details never leave your device. We don't store, transmit, or have access to any of your financial information.

Can I use this for business income?

This calculator is designed for calculating tax on salary and other common income sources. For complex business income with expenses, depreciation, and other business-specific deductions, we recommend consulting a tax professional as business taxation has additional complexities.
