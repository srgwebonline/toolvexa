Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between km/h and mph?

Kilometers per hour (km/h) measures the number of kilometers traveled in one hour, while miles per hour (mph) measures the number of miles traveled in one hour. 1 mph equals approximately 1.60934 km/h. Km/h is used in most countries worldwide, while mph is primarily used in the United States, United Kingdom, and a few other countries.

What are knots and when are they used?

Knots (kn) are a unit of speed equal to one nautical mile per hour. One knot is approximately 1.852 km/h or 1.15078 mph. Knots are primarily used in aviation, maritime navigation, and meteorology because they relate directly to latitude and longitude measurements on nautical charts.

How fast is Mach 1?

Mach 1 is the speed of sound, which varies depending on temperature and altitude. At sea level at 15°C (59°F), Mach 1 is approximately 1,225 km/h (761 mph or 340.3 m/s). At higher altitudes where the air is colder, the speed of sound decreases. Mach numbers are used in aviation and aerodynamics to express speeds relative to the speed of sound.

What is the Beaufort scale?

The Beaufort scale is an empirical measure that relates wind speed to observed conditions at sea or on land. It ranges from 0 (calm) to 12 (hurricane force). While not a precise measurement like km/h or mph, it provides a standardized way to estimate wind speed based on visual observations. Our converter includes approximate km/h and mph equivalents for each Beaufort level.

Why are there different speed units?

Different speed units developed in different contexts: mph from the British imperial system, km/h from the metric system, knots from navigation, m/s from scientific measurement, etc. Each unit system serves specific needs - nautical miles (and thus knots) relate to geographical coordinates, while km/h and mph are practical for everyday travel. Our converter helps bridge these different measurement systems.

How accurate is this speed converter?

Our speed converter uses internationally accepted conversion factors with high precision. For most practical purposes, the conversions are exact. For scientific calculations requiring extreme precision, note that some conversion factors (like the speed of light) are defined exactly, while others (like mph to km/h) use fixed conversion factors accurate to many decimal places. All calculations are performed with JavaScript's full floating-point precision.
