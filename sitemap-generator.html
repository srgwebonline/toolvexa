Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sitemap and why do I need one?

A sitemap is a file that lists all the important pages of your website, helping search engines discover and index your content. XML sitemaps are for search engines, while HTML sitemaps are for website visitors. Sitemaps improve SEO by ensuring all your pages are found and indexed properly.

Is the Sitemap Generator really free to use?

Yes, absolutely! Our Sitemap Generator is 100% free with no hidden costs, limitations, or watermarks. You can generate as many sitemaps as you need without any restrictions. We believe in providing valuable SEO tools to help everyone improve their website visibility.

What's the difference between XML and HTML sitemaps?

XML sitemaps are designed for search engines - they help Google, Bing, and other search engines discover and index your pages. HTML sitemaps are for human visitors - they provide a clear overview of your site structure and help users navigate your website. Both are important for SEO and user experience.

How many URLs should my sitemap contain?

Google recommends keeping sitemaps under 50,000 URLs and 50MB in size. For most websites, a sitemap with 50-500 URLs is sufficient. Our tool automatically adjusts the size based on your selection and ensures your sitemap follows SEO best practices.

Where should I submit my sitemap?

After generating your sitemap, you should:

  1. Upload the XML file to your website's root directory
  2. Submit the sitemap URL to Google Search Console
  3. Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools
  4. Add the HTML sitemap to your website's navigation or footer

Will this tool crawl my actual website?

No, our tool doesn't actually crawl your website. We generate sitemaps based on common website structures and the options you select. This ensures privacy and speed - your website isn't accessed, and you get instant results. The generated sitemap serves as a template that you can customize with your actual URLs.
