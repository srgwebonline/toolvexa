Frequently Asked Questions

How is the monthly payment calculated?

The monthly payment is calculated using the standard loan amortization formula: M = P [ i(1 + i)^n ] / [ (1 + i)^n - 1 ], where M is the monthly payment, P is the loan principal, i is the monthly interest rate (APR/12), and n is the number of months.

Should I make a larger down payment?

A larger down payment reduces your loan amount, which lowers your monthly payment and total interest paid. It can also help you avoid being "upside down" on your loan (owing more than the car is worth). Aim for at least 20% down payment when possible.

What is an amortization schedule?

An amortization schedule shows how each payment is split between principal and interest over the life of the loan. In the early years, most of your payment goes toward interest. As the loan matures, more of each payment goes toward reducing the principal balance.

How does the loan term affect my payment?

Longer loan terms (e.g., 72 months vs. 36 months) result in lower monthly payments but higher total interest costs. Shorter terms mean higher monthly payments but less interest paid overall. Choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford.

Are the calculations accurate for my situation?

This calculator provides estimates based on standard auto loan calculations. Actual loan terms may vary based on your credit score, lender policies, and other factors. Always consult with your lender for exact figures before making financial decisions.
