Frequently Asked Questions

How does the PDF to Word conversion work?

Our tool extracts text, images, tables, and formatting from your PDF file and recreates them in Microsoft Word format. The conversion happens entirely in your browser using advanced JavaScript algorithms, ensuring your files never leave your device.

Will my formatting be preserved?

Yes, our converter does its best to preserve fonts, colors, images, tables, and layout. However, complex PDFs with intricate formatting may require minor adjustments in Word. Simple text-based PDFs convert with near-perfect formatting retention.

Is there a file size limit?

No, there's no file size limit for conversion. However, very large files may take longer to process. For optimal performance, we recommend converting files under 10 MB, but the tool can handle larger files as well.

Are my files secure?

Absolutely! All conversion happens locally in your browser. Your files never leave your device or get uploaded to any server. This ensures complete privacy and security for your documents.

What's the difference between DOC and DOCX formats?

DOC is the older binary format used by Word 97-2003, while DOCX is the newer XML-based format introduced with Word 2007. DOCX offers better compression, improved recovery of corrupted files, and generally better compatibility with modern software. We recommend using DOCX unless you specifically need compatibility with very old Word versions.

Can I convert scanned PDFs?

For scanned PDFs (image-based), our tool will convert them to Word but the text won't be editable unless it has been OCR-processed. For best results with scanned documents, use an OCR (Optical Character Recognition) tool first, then convert to Word.
