Frequently Asked Questions

How does compound interest work in investments?

Compound interest is when you earn interest on both your initial investment and the interest that accumulates over time. This creates exponential growth, as each period's interest earns additional interest in subsequent periods. The formula used is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the future value, P is principal, r is annual rate, n is compounding periods per year, and t is years.

What's a realistic expected annual return?

Historical stock market returns average about 7-10% annually after inflation. Conservative portfolios might yield 4-6%, while aggressive growth strategies could target 8-12%. Remember that past performance doesn't guarantee future results, and all investments carry risk. Diversification and long-term perspective are key to managing investment risk.

How important are regular contributions to investments?

Regular contributions (dollar-cost averaging) are crucial for long-term investment success. They allow you to benefit from market fluctuations by buying more shares when prices are low and fewer when prices are high. Over time, this strategy can significantly reduce the average cost per share and increase your overall returns.

Should I adjust for inflation in my calculations?

For accurate long-term planning, it's recommended to use real returns (nominal returns minus inflation) rather than nominal returns. Historical inflation averages 2-3% annually. If you use nominal returns in this calculator, your results will be in today's dollars. For inflation-adjusted results, subtract expected inflation from your expected return rate.

Are the calculations accurate for tax-advantaged accounts?

This calculator shows pre-tax investment growth. For tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s or IRAs, the results reflect tax-deferred growth. For taxable accounts, you'd need to account for taxes on dividends and capital gains, which would reduce net returns. Consult with a tax professional for specific tax implications of your investments.
