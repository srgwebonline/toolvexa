Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the investment calculator for Indian investors?

Our investment calculator uses precise financial formulas to provide accurate projections for Indian investors. It accounts for rupee-based calculations and typical Indian investment scenarios including SIP and lumpsum investments.

What's the difference between SIP and lumpsum investment?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) involves investing a fixed amount regularly (monthly), while lumpsum is a one-time investment. SIP helps in rupee cost averaging and is ideal for most Indian investors. Our calculator supports both scenarios.

What is a realistic return rate for Indian investments?

For equity mutual funds in India, a realistic long-term return expectation is 12-15% annually. For debt funds, 6-8%. FD rates vary between 5-7%. Adjust the calculator based on your investment type and risk appetite.

Are taxes considered in the calculations?

No, this calculator shows pre-tax returns. For accurate post-tax returns, you should deduct applicable taxes (like capital gains tax for equity or TDS for FDs) from the final amount based on your tax slab.

Is my financial information stored or shared?

No! All calculations happen locally in your browser. We don't store, transmit, or collect any of your financial information. Your privacy and financial data security are completely protected.

Technical Information

The tool uses the compound interest formula adapted for monthly SIPs:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) + PMT × (((1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1) / (r/n))

Where:
A = Future value of investment in ₹
P = Principal investment amount in ₹
r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
n = Number of times interest compounds per year
t = Time in years
PMT = Monthly SIP contribution in ₹
