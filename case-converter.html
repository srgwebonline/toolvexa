Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Title Case and Sentence Case?

Title Case capitalizes the first letter of every major word (nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and some conjunctions). Sentence Case only capitalizes the first letter of the first word in each sentence and proper nouns.

Example:
Title Case: "The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over The Lazy Dog"
Sentence Case: "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

What are camelCase, PascalCase, snake_case, and kebab-case used for?

These cases are commonly used in programming:

  • camelCase: Used for variable and function names in JavaScript, Java, etc. (example: "myVariableName")
  • PascalCase: Used for class names in many programming languages (example: "MyClassName")
  • snake_case: Used in Python, Ruby, and databases (example: "my_variable_name")
  • kebab-case: Used in URLs, CSS classes, and some configurations (example: "my-variable-name")
Is my text safe when using this converter?

Yes, absolutely! All text conversion happens 100% locally in your browser. Your text never leaves your device or gets sent to any server. We don't store, track, or analyze any text you input. This ensures complete privacy and security for your content.

Can I convert large documents with this tool?

Yes, the Case Converter can handle large amounts of text. However, for optimal performance, we recommend converting text in sections if you're working with very large documents (10,000+ words). The tool works best with documents up to 50,000 characters, which is approximately 10,000 words.

Does the converter preserve formatting and special characters?

The converter preserves line breaks, paragraph breaks, and most special characters. However, some special formatting (like bold, italics, or font colors) from rich text editors may not be preserved as this is a plain text converter. For best results, paste plain text without formatting.
