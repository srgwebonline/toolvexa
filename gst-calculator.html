Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between GST exclusive and inclusive?

GST Exclusive: The amount entered does NOT include GST. GST will be added to this amount to calculate the total payable amount.

GST Inclusive: The amount entered already INCLUDES GST. The calculator will separate the GST amount from the total to show the base price and GST separately.

What are CGST, SGST, and IGST?

CGST (Central GST): Collected by the Central Government on intra-state transactions.

SGST (State GST): Collected by the State Government on intra-state transactions.

IGST (Integrated GST): Collected by the Central Government on inter-state transactions.

For example, at 18% GST rate: Intra-state = 9% CGST + 9% SGST, Inter-state = 18% IGST.

What GST rates are applicable in India?

India has four primary GST slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Some essential items are taxed at 0% (nil rate), while special rates apply to precious metals, luxury items, and sin goods. The exact rate depends on the HSN code of the product or service.

How do I calculate GST manually?

For GST Exclusive Amount: GST = Original Amount × (GST Rate / 100), Total = Original Amount + GST

For GST Inclusive Amount: Original Amount = Total Amount ÷ (1 + GST Rate / 100), GST = Total Amount - Original Amount

Example for ₹1000 at 18% GST exclusive: GST = ₹180, Total = ₹1180
