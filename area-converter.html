Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between square feet and square meters?

Square feet (ft²) is an imperial/US customary unit of area, while square meters (m²) is the metric unit. One square meter equals approximately 10.764 square feet. Square meters are used worldwide in most countries, while square feet are primarily used in the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom.

How many square feet are in an acre?

One acre equals 43,560 square feet. An acre is a unit of area commonly used in land measurement, especially in agriculture and real estate. It's approximately 40% of a hectare. Historically, an acre was defined as the area that could be plowed in one day by a yoke of oxen.

What is a hectare and when is it used?

A hectare (ha) is a metric unit of area equal to 10,000 square meters or approximately 2.471 acres. It's commonly used for measuring land areas, particularly in agriculture, forestry, and land planning. Hectares are the standard unit for land measurement in most countries outside the United States.

How do I convert square meters to square feet?

To convert square meters to square feet, multiply the square meter value by 10.7639. For example, 100 square meters = 100 × 10.7639 = 1,076.39 square feet. Our converter automates this calculation with precise conversion factors for accurate results every time.

What are Bigha and Gunta units?

Bigha and Gunta are traditional units of area measurement used in India and neighboring countries. The size of a Bigha varies by region (typically 1,600 to 6,771 m²). Gunta is approximately 101.17 m² or 1,089 ft². These units are still commonly used in real estate transactions in rural areas of South Asia.

Why are accurate area conversions important?

Accurate area conversions are crucial for property transactions, construction projects, agricultural planning, legal documentation, and academic work. Incorrect conversions can lead to financial losses, construction errors, legal disputes, or academic inaccuracies. Our tool ensures precision using standardized conversion factors.
